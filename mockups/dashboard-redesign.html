Dashboard redesign · concept pitch

Built around the fridge and the garage

The current dashboard shows one battery, one input number, one output number — it treats every watt the same. But the two things that actually matter here are keeping the fridge cold and running the garage. These three directions reorganise the screen around those two jobs: the fridge as a protected, runtime-first load, and the garage as a live, hands-on console.

01 — the everyday view

Priority Zones

Two cards, ranked by what you worry about.

The screen leads with the fridge because that's the question you open the app to answer: how long until it's at risk? A single reassuring number — cooling time left — sits above a battery bar with a marked reserve floor, so "safe" is a state you can see, not a percentage you have to interpret.

  • Fridge card is runtime-first: big time-to-reserve, a SAFE pill, and a lock showing its output is protected from accidental toggles.
  • Garage card is control-first: live draw, per-output switches, and current charge-in right where your thumb is.
  • Everything else — total flow, SOC ring, today's kWh — collapses into one quiet strip at the bottom.

02 — the mental model

Split Rail

One battery, two rails you can reason about.

Same single power station, drawn the way you actually think about it: the pack feeds two separate rails. The fridge rail is thin and protected; the garage rail is fat and busy. Live dashes show power moving so you can tell charge from drain at a glance.

  • A reserve line on the pack makes the safety margin literal — garage loads shed before they can eat into the fridge's hours.
  • Each rail owns its own runtime, so you never have to do the "but what's left for the fridge?" math yourself.
  • Turns a raw net-watts number into a decision: keep working, or ease off.

03 — the across-the-room view

Glance Board

Big tiles for a phone propped up in the garage.

Built for distance, not detail. When the phone is charging on the workbench and the fridge is humming three metres away, you want two colours and two numbers: is the fridge green, and how hard is the garage pulling? Everything reads in a half-second glance.

  • Two oversized hero tiles carry the fridge and garage; four supporting tiles fill the gaps without competing.
  • State is colour — green fridge means cold and safe, amber garage means working — legible before you read a digit.
  • Each tile is a tap target, so the glance view is also the fastest way to flip an output.

Interpretation: fridge = protected, runtime-first load; garage = live, hands-on console. All three share the app's native palette and the F2400 scenario shown (74% · +410 W in · 722 W out). Numbers are illustrative — nothing here writes to a device.
